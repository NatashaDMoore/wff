Frequently Asked Questions

  • What should I bring to each game?

    We recommend wearing clothes you are comfortable running in and bringing water to stay hydrated during the game. Many players purchase cleats and football receiver's gloves to improve their play but these items are not required. Each team must provide their own ball. Coordinating with your team ahead of game day is helpful to ensure you are prepared.

  • What does the league provide?

    The league provides referees to manage timekeeping, scorekeeping, and penalties. Flags will be provided as well as cones for marking the boundaries of the field.

  • What ball size should I use?

    The game ball must be, at minimum, the size of a Youth Football (Ages 12-14). Teams may choose to use an adult-sized football. The Referee will be the sole judge of any ball offered for play.

  • Are there uniforms?

    Some teams have custom jerseys but most teams will wear either light or dark shirts during the game. Players of opposing teams must wear contrasting colored jerseys/t-shirts. Numbers on the back of shirts are not required. If both teams have similar colored jerseys, the visiting team must make any uniform changes required unless the home team has secondary jerseys.

  • How big is the field we play on?

    We set up three side-by-side fields on a football field. The width of the field will be 25 yards. The front of the endzone will be the football sideline and the back will be the soccer sideline.

  • Is this contact football?

    No intentional contact is allowed. Incidental contact is allowable in situations where a player is making a play on the ball, attempting to pull the opposing player's flag, or in a stationary position, such as setting a screen.

  • Can I rush the quarterback?

    Yes. All defensive players that rush the passer must be a minimum of 7 yards from the line of scrimmage (designated by the referee) when the ball is snapped. Any number of players can rush the passer as long as they are beyond the 7-yard mark.

    The rusher must be at least 1 yard off to either side when starting her rush. The rusher must not be “head up” with the center else she receives a penalty. Penalty: Defensive offsides - 5 yds previous spot of the ball
